Why a whole site about parking?

Because in Southern California's mountains, the trailhead lot — not the trail — is what ruins plans. Lots built for a quieter era meet Instagram-era demand, shoulders get patrolled, and "just park anywhere" turns into a citation or a tow. Each guide here covers one trailhead with the numbers that matter: how many cars fit, when it fills on a weekend, what pass goes on the dash, and the realistic plan B.

Coverage now spans four Southern California ranges: the San Bernardinos (Lake Arrowhead–Crestline–Running Springs, Big Bear, Forest Falls), the San Gabriels (Chantry Flat, Switzer, East Fork, Icehouse Canyon), the San Jacintos (Idyllwild), and San Diego County (Cedar Creek Falls, Three Sisters, Mt. Woodson, Torrey Pines). Every fact is checked against official sources — Forest Service pages, state and city parks, county sites, operators — and date-stamped, including the fire and heat closures that decide whether the drive is worth making at all. For everything beyond parking, see our sister guides LakeArrowheadMap.com and BigBearMap.com.
