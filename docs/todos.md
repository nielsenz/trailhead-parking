# Open items

Unresolved facts, contradictions between official sources, and things that will
go stale. Each entry is dated when logged. **Resolve by fixing the page, not by
defending it** — and re-stamp that page's `verified` date when you do.

Convention: `[ ]` open, `[x]` resolved (keep it, with the resolution date and
what the answer turned out to be — the history is useful when a source flips
back).

### [ ] Chantry Flat road status is volatile
**Logged 2026-07-20.** Open as of July 2026 (verified against the USFS page),
but the road is managed by three entities — City of Sierra Madre, LA County
Public Works, and the Angeles NF — and USFS states it "may be closed to vehicles
for safety reasons at any time in this steep environment, even if the picnic
area and adjacent hiking trails are open."

Reopened Oct 2024 after the Bobcat Fire, closed again Jan 2025 for the Eaton
Fire, reopened April 2025. The page tells readers to call **(818) 899-1900** the
morning of. Expect this to need re-checking every few months.

### [ ] Eaton Fire closure runs through Dec 31, 2027 — watch for early reopening
**Logged 2026-07-20.** Area Closure Order 05-01-26-04 covers Eaton Canyon, Echo
Mountain, Mount Wilson Toll Road, Idlehour, Castle Canyon, Sam Merrill and Mount
Lowe. Build those pages when a reopening is announced, ahead of the demand
spike — this is a known-date content opportunity, not just a caveat.

The Sierra Madre section of the Mt. Wilson Trail is a separate matter: the lower
~3.5 miles were rebuilt and reopened (the race returned May 2026), the upper
section is still closed.

## Contradictions between official sources

Each of these has two live, official, mutually inconsistent pages. The site
currently presents both and tells the reader how to behave. A phone call settles
each one.

### [ ] East Fork: is the wilderness permit required or voluntary?
**Logged 2026-07-20.** The USFS *trail* page says required; the USFS *parking*
page says "encouraged but strictly voluntary." Both live. The page presents the
contradiction and advises self-registering anyway.
**Settle at (626) 335-1251.**

### [ ] Cucamonga Wilderness permit: which office issues it?
**Logged 2026-07-20.** The SBNF page routes through SGWA online (90 days out);
SoCalHiker and others say Lytle Creek Ranger Station. The Icehouse Canyon page
presents both routes. Not resolvable from official sources alone.
**Try (909) 382-2851.**

### [ ] Switzer: when does the lower spur gate actually close?
**Logged 2026-07-20.** USFS lists day use as 6am–10pm; tchester.org says the
spur gate closes at 6pm. The page notes both and advises parking at the highway
lot for a late finish. A 4-hour discrepancy is the difference between a walk out
and a towed car.

### [ ] Torrey Pines: South Beach fee ceiling
**Logged 2026-07-20.** Two CA State Parks pages disagree — the fee page
(`?page_id=31836`) says $12–$20, the `parks.ca.gov/torreypines` landing page
says $12–$25. Page covers both with "from $12, up to $25 at peak."

### [ ] Ernie Maxwell permit status (Idyllwild)
**Logged 2026-07-18, carried over.** One contradictory USFS webpage; the
brochure says no permit required. Confirm at **(909) 382-2921** if challenged.

## Numbers we could not source

The editorial rule is to never invent a capacity. These pages describe their
lots qualitatively instead. If an official count ever appears, add it.

**Logged 2026-07-20.** East Fork, Chantry Flat, Switzer and Icehouse Canyon.
USFS says only "space is limited" / "parking is limited." Switzer's `lot_size`
cites the verifiable "26 picnic sites, one ADA space" instead of a car count.

### [ ] Three Sisters' 80 vehicles is design capacity, not a count
**Logged 2026-07-20.** The figure comes from the National Forest Foundation's
project description (the partner, not the agency) and describes the rebuilt
trailhead as designed, not as counted after construction. Attributed inline on
the page. A USFS figure would be better.

### [ ] Cedar Creek Falls lot capacity
**Logged 2026-07-20.** No official number. Hiker reports consistently describe
roughly a dozen marked spaces; the page attributes the range rather than
stating it. Note the permit cap, not the lot, is the binding constraint.

**Logged 2026-07-20.** Traces to launch-era reporting (East County Magazine)
and hiking guides. The page calls it "well-sourced but unconfirmed on the
official page." Worth confirming so it can be stated plainly.

### [ ] Chantry Flat lot count
**Logged 2026-07-20.** One secondary source says three lots; USFS says "both
parking lots." Page uses the USFS-consistent "upper and lower paved lots plus
road spaces."
